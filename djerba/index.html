Discover Djerba

The Island

Djerba is the largest island off North Africa, located in the Gulf of Gabès on Tunisia's eastern coast. Known as the "Island of Dreams," it covers 514 square kilometers of pure Mediterranean magic.

Perfect Climate

With over 300 days of sunshine per year, Djerba offers a mild Mediterranean climate. Warm summers and gentle winters make it a year-round destination for travelers seeking paradise.

Ancient History

Home to one of the oldest Jewish communities in the world, Djerba has been a crossroads of civilizations for millennia. From Phoenicians to Romans, each culture left its mark.

Essential Information

About Djerba

Djerba, the largest island in North Africa off Tunisia's southeastern coast in Gabes Bay, is best known as a popular tourist destination with its beautiful beaches, rich cultural heritage (archaeological sites, craft villages) and vibrant nightlife. It is also famous for its unique Barbarian accent and history stretching from antiquity (possible home of Ulisses) to modern times, with well developed tourism infrastructure of luxury resorts and hotels.

Background Information

  • Location: Southeast of Tunisia, in the Gulf of Gabes
  • Area: About 514 km²
  • Main City: Houmt Souk (Market Hummus)
  • Population: More than 165,000 people
  • Connection: Road network, ferries, and international airport

Rich Culture

Traditional Architecture

White-washed houses with blue doors, ancient mosques, and the famous El Ghriba Synagogue showcase Djerba's unique architectural heritage blending Arab, Jewish, and Mediterranean influences.

Vibrant Markets

The souks of Houmt Souk burst with colors, spices, and handmade crafts. From traditional pottery to woven textiles, every corner tells a story of artisan mastery passed through generations.
