The Island

Djerba is the largest island off North Africa, located in the Gulf of Gab√®s on Tunisia's eastern coast. Known as the "Island of Dreams," it covers 514 square kilometers of pure Mediterranean magic.

Perfect Climate

With over 300 days of sunshine per year, Djerba offers a mild Mediterranean climate. Warm summers and gentle winters make it a year-round destination for travelers seeking paradise.

Ancient History

Home to one of the oldest Jewish communities in the world, Djerba has been a crossroads of civilizations for millennia. From Phoenicians to Romans, each culture left its mark.

Rich Culture

Traditional Architecture

White-washed houses with blue doors, ancient mosques, and the famous El Ghriba Synagogue showcase Djerba's unique architectural heritage blending Arab, Jewish, and Mediterranean influences.

Vibrant Markets

The souks of Houmt Souk burst with colors, spices, and handmade crafts. From traditional pottery to woven textiles, every corner tells a story of artisan mastery passed through generations.
